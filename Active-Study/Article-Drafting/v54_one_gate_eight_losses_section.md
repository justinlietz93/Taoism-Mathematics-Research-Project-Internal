# Section: One Gate, Eight Losses (v54)

*Role in the larger piece.* This is the synthesis section and the last of the evidence sections. Every
section before it examined one witness, internal or external. This one steps back and asks what they
have in common, and answers with a single gate and a typed catalogue of the ways that gate can fail.
Its central work is to make precise the claim the whole essay has been guarding: that the bridges
cohere without collapsing into the false statement that these systems are the same system. It consumes
the running table from the outside-witness section and the individual results from every section
since, and it produces the one sentence and the one taxonomy that the closing assessment will judge.
It belongs after the replay capstone and immediately before the closing.

---

## The one sentence

After every witness, the rule the essay has been circling can be stated once, in final form:

> A projection is bridge-admissible only when it preserves enough retained state to determine the next
> admissible transition under the relevant operator family.

That is the gate. A readout, a label, a scalar, an image, a name, is admissible as a stand-in for the
state only if you could continue correctly from it alone. If you cannot, it is a shadow, and the thing
that casts it is the state. Every section in the essay was an instance of this one sentence, and the
sentence covers all of them without once asserting that any two corpora are identical.

## Eight ways to lose

The synthesis sorts every witness by what kind of state its tempting readout throws away. There are
eight distinct classes, and each has a concrete witness already established in an earlier section:

| Loss class | Witness | What the projection collides | Minimum fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| order loss | non-commutative commutator | the swap and the identity share a visible projection | keep the order, or the central charge |
| history loss | monodromy double cover | the empty loop and the two-turn loop share a visible sheet | keep the winding and the loop history |
| action-registry loss | five-sheet branch grammar | the same word maps to different sheet actions | keep the generator and action registry |
| basis loss | Pencil Yin-Yang vector transfer | the same components in different chart bases | keep the chart basis and channel type |
| digit-order loss | Ancient Yi octal readout | the scalar loses the reading convention | keep the least-significant-first order and place width |
| semantic-readout loss | Wilhelm line corpus | the line text does not fix the transition | keep the six-line carrier and selected line |
| diagnostic loss | MFE / Liu projections | the image does not fix the full field state | keep the field, boundary callbacks, connectivity |
| stale-selector loss | the demoted reset operator | the selector resets or compresses state | separate the witness layer from the corrected canon |

The teaching image: a relay race. The gate is "do not drop the baton," where the baton is whatever the
next runner needs to keep going. There are many ways to drop it. You can fumble the handoff, which is
basis loss. You can pass it in the wrong order, which is order loss. You can lose track of how many
laps have been run, which is history loss. You can lose the rulebook that says what each runner does,
which is registry loss. Every one of these is a dropped baton, the single gate failing, but the manner
of dropping differs, and the manner is diagnostic of the system. A code drops it one way, a divination
corpus another, a plasma simulation a third. The gate is shared. The failure mode is a fingerprint.

## Coherence without identity

This is the structural payoff and the section's reason to exist. The oldest objection to work that
links ancient and modern systems is that it manufactures unity by selection, finding resemblances and
declaring them deep. The taxonomy is the precise refutation, and the refutation has two halves that
must be held together.

The bridges cohere, because every corpus obeys the same gate. That is not a resemblance between
images or vocabularies. It is the same functional rule, that a stand-in is valid only if you can
continue from it, holding across a plasma code, a divination automaton, a number system, and a
non-commutative geometry.

The bridges do not collapse into identity, because each corpus loses a different class of state. The I
Ching is not the Pencil code. Ancient Yi is not the quintic. They are distinct systems that happen to
be subject to one law and that violate it in their own characteristic ways. This is exactly how shared
structure looks when it is real rather than imagined. A pendulum, a spring, and a planet's orbit all
obey one conservation law without anyone claiming a pendulum is a planet. The law is shared; the
systems remain themselves. Motif-matching claims surface identity and selects its evidence. This claims
a shared gate, admits the systems are different, names eight distinct failure modes, and ships
executable negative controls that try to break the gate on each. That is the opposite procedure, and
it lands in the opposite place.

This also answers, at the structural level, the question the whole investigation was built around.
The fingerprint of a genuine underlying structure is that independent systems, which could not have
copied one another, turn out to obey the same law while staying distinct. The taxonomy is that
fingerprint made into a table: one gate, many corpora, eight losses, no forced identity.

## The minimal-state kernel

The roadmap table in the outside-witness section gave one side of this: for each corpus, the readout
that looks complete and the state it quietly drops. The synthesis closes the other side, the positive
specification of what the full retained state actually is in each system. A compressed reading of that
positive column:

```
Corrected Orthad   q, theta, rank, axes, emitted word, lap index
CF19 / Farey / Xi  pair, floor, articulation, theta, kappa
Monodromy          sheet, winding, history, action registry
Branch grammar     generator and action registry
NC geometry        order, and the hidden central charge
Ancient Yi         carrier, digit order, place width
Wilhelm            carrier, selected line, reading order
Pencil Yin-Yang    chart, basis, channel registry
MFE / Liu          field, boundary callbacks, connectivity
```

Set this beside the roadmap table and the synthesis is complete: that table names the shadow each
system casts, this one names the body that casts it, and the gate is the relation between them. The
two together, the dropped readout and the state it was a shadow of, are the whole content of the
state-completeness rule, closed now over every corpus the essay treats.

## What this section contributes, and what stays open

Contributes to the merged draft: the final one-sentence statement of the gate; the eight-class loss
taxonomy that turns a pile of witnesses into a structured argument; the explicit, defensible statement
of coherence without identity, which is the essay's answer to its oldest objection; and the closed
minimal-state kernel across all corpora.

Stays open, and the synthesis says so in its own status rather than rounding up: the Ancient Yi
philological line-order proof, the Liu 2022 plasma time series, local proof compilation, the latest
active sources on local storage, and, most pointedly, the Shadow modular residual, which remains an
external reference and is not closed inside the lift. The synthesis reports the overall pass as high
and explicitly not total, and lists the blockers as real rather than cosmetic.

A guard to preserve through the merge: the value of this section is the conjunction, coherence and
non-identity together. Drop the first half and the essay looks like a loose collection of analogies.
Drop the second half and it looks like the overreach it has spent every section avoiding. Both halves,
stated together, are the position.

*Hands off to.* The evidence is now assembled, sorted, and bounded. One gate, eight losses, a closed
kernel, and an honest list of what remains open. The gate has already been rooted in the admissible
distinction, in the opening section. One thing remains: to say plainly what the whole convergence does
and does not establish. That is the closing assessment, and it is where the essay ends.
