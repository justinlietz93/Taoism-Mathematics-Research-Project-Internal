# Phase 5 v1 — Primitive-to-Weil Generation Attempt

## Claim under attack

The discrete retained-orientation primitive generates the modular machinery. The Weil representation, the S/T transformation law, and the weight structure are consequences of Q and B/L, derived from the primitives, not calibrated against classical modular theory.

## Verdict

`BROKE_AT_PRIMITIVE_TO_GENERATOR_JOIN`

The exact native primitives do not generate the Weil representation under the non-retuning constraint.

## Source definitions used

The same primitives from the prior packages are used:

```text
Q(A,q,θ,κ,c) = (A,q,θ+π/2,floor((θ+π/2)/(2π)),c+π/2)

B(A,(u,v),θ,κ,c) = (A,sort(v,u+v),θ,κ,C(θ,1/(v(u+v))))

L(A,q,θ,κ,c) = (N(A),q,θ+π/2,floor((θ+π/2)/(2π)),c+π/2)
```

Ancient Yi uses retained LSD-first successor/carry. Wilhelm uses selected-line flip, with line 6 given by `x xor 32`.

## Required generators

For `A_m = Z/(2m)Z`, `N=2m`:

```text
T_r = exp(π i r²/N)
S_rs = N^(-1/2) exp(-2π i r s/N)
```

## Join 1: Q to T

Native Q advances visible phase by `π/2`, so its visible action is constant multiplication by `i`. Q² gives constant multiplication by `-1`.

Weil T is not constant. It is residue-quadratic:

```text
T_r = exp(π i r²/N)
```

Therefore Q and Q² do not equal T value-for-value on either the χ12 carrier (`N=12`) or the χ8 carrier (`N=8`).

## Join 2: B/L to S

Native B is a state-local refinement of an ordered pair:

```text
(u,v) -> sort(v,u+v)
```

Native L is host lift/phase translation with q carried.

Neither operation is a dense linear transform over functions on `Z/NZ`. The Weil S generator is dense:

```text
S_rs = N^(-1/2) exp(-2π i r s/N)
```

Ancient Yi carry and Wilhelm line flip are sparse permutations. A sparse deterministic transition is not the dense finite Fourier transform. The Fourier transform may diagonalize a carry/permutation action, or appear at a representation/projection layer, but it is not equal to the primitive transition itself.

## Checks against χ12 and χ8

The explicit Weil S/T matrices reproduce the known multiplier surfaces for `N=12` and `N=8` as matrices. That confirms the target representation is constructed correctly. It also isolates the failure: the target representation is not generated by the current native Q/B/L definitions without an added dual-seating kernel.

## Falsifier verdict

The Phase 5 falsifier fires:

```text
If B/L dual seating, taken from its own primitive definition, is any pairing other than the finite Fourier transform on the residue carrier, the generative claim fails at that join.
```

The pairing actually present in the native definitions is not the finite Fourier pairing `rs/N`. It is a collection of deterministic retained-state transitions:

```text
Phase/Farey:     local pair refinement (u,v)->(v,u+v)
Ancient Yi:      LSD-first successor/carry
Wilhelm:         selected-line flip; line6 xor32
Shadow carrier:  finite Fourier kernel only after an added residue dual-pairing layer
```
