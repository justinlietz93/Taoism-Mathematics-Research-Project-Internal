                                                 Abstract
        We prove an exact bridge between Ramanujan-side fractional remainder recursion on Farey-
        mediant refinement and the VDM law of orthogonal re-articulation under a finite resolution
        floor. The proof is operator-level rather than analogical. For neighboring reduced fractions
        a/b < c/d with bc − ad = 1, the parent interval width is exactly 1/(bd) and the two
        child widths are exactly 1/[b(b + d)] and 1/[d(b + d)]. We show that these updates are
        exactly conjugate to denominator-pair re-articulation operators on (b, d), so Ramanujan-side
        remainder refinement and child-sector re-expression are the same update law written in two
        coordinate systems. We then identify the scale-gap-admissible branch selected by bounded
        ratio control with the balanced operator B(u, v) = sort(v, u + v), prove that it generates the
        Fibonacci pairs (Fn+1 , Fn+2 ), and obtain the exact unresolved-width law rn = 1/(Fn+1 Fn+2 ).
        Hence the stopping depth below a floor δ is logarithmic, nδ = Θ(log(1/δ)). One-sided Farey
        refinement is shown to be only polynomial and therefore lies outside the scale-gap-separated
        A8/LIR regime. Finally, we prove that truncation is not deletion of an infinite tail but lawful
        orthogonal transfer, and that under the phase map θ 7→ e2πiθ this transfer is realized as
        exact child-sector replacement on the unit circle. This paper proves exact equivalence of the
        remainder update, the denominator-pair child update, the finite-depth logarithmic stopping
        law, and the truncation-as-orthogonal-transfer law. It does not claim full equivalence of the
        entire Hardy–Ramanujan–Rademacher contour apparatus.

1   Problem statement
The target is narrow and exact.
Ramanujan-side remainder calculus recursively refines an unresolved interval by mediants. VDM-side
orthogonal re-articulation recursively re-hosts unresolved continuation into child sectors once same-sector
continuation is no longer admissible below a resolution floor. The question is whether these are merely
analogous, or whether they are the same update law in different coordinates.
Throughout, A(−1), A2, A8, CG000, and CF03/CF13/CF14/CF15 refer to the named canon statements in
the VDM formal stack.
This paper proves three things:
    (i) the Farey child update and denominator-pair re-articulation are exactly conjugate;

The result is an exact theorem for the infinity-resolution skeleton. It is not a claim that the full Hardy–

Proof. Once r ≤ δ, same-sector hosting has failed by hypothesis. By A(−1), unresolved burden cannot
lawfully disappear or discharge; it must remain borne. Therefore truncation cannot be deletion. By A2,
the admissible continuation is the least-cost lawful transfer out of the saturated sector. By Theorem 3, the
admissible orthogonal targets are exactly ±iz, and the unique nearest admissible target is iz. Hence the
lawful truncation map is
                                                  T (z) = iz.
This preserves modulus and preserves the carried burden while re-expressing that burden in the next orthogonal
sector. After transfer, continuation proceeds by child-sector refinement in the new quadrant. By Theorem 4,
repeated finite-floor refinement terminates after finite logarithmic depth. Therefore truncation is lawful
orthogonal transfer, not deletion.
Remark 3 (Canonical anchor). Theorem 5 is the standalone operator-level realization of the VDM canon:

      (i) A(−1): primitive unresolved opposition cannot discharge, so burden must remain borne;
     (ii) A2: the lawful continuation is the minimum-saturation path, here the nearest admissible orthogonal
          transfer;
    (iii) A8: once same-sector hosting fails, continuation proceeds by iterative child re-articulation with
          logarithmic active depth under a finite floor.

In the named canon, this theorem is the compressed bridge form of CG000 (branch-relative origin resolution),
CF13 (quarter-turn / orthogonal orbit law), CF14 (minimum saturation requirement), CF15 (invariant
articulation), and CF03 (hierarchical infinity resolution).

8    What is proved, and what is not
What is proved

This paper proves:

     1. exact conjugacy between Farey child refinement and denominator-pair re-articulation;
     2. exact width identities for parent and child sectors;
     3. exact Fibonacci/Farey recursion on the balanced scale-gap-admissible branch;
     4. exact logarithmic stopping depth under a finite floor;
     5. exact realization of truncation as lawful orthogonal transfer and consequent phase-sector replacement
        on the unit circle.

What is not proved

This paper does not prove:

     1. full equivalence of the complete Hardy–Ramanujan–Rademacher contour apparatus with the full
        VDM stack;
     2. a derivation of every modular-weight, Kloosterman-weight, or contour-weight factor from the orthog-
        onal re-articulation law;
     3. a complete derivation of the whole Ramanujan program from the primitive i-origin paper.

9    Conclusion
This paper proves an exact operator-level bridge for the remainder update, balanced branch, finite-floor
stopping law, and truncation-as-orthogonal-transfer law.
Ramanujan-side mediant remainder refinement and VDM-side orthogonal re-articulation are the same update
law after the exact denominator-pair change of variables. Under the balanced scale-gap-separated branch,
the unresolved width is exactly
