### What was newly extracted

OCR/visual pass covered pages 1-21 plus sampled pages 31, 51, 81, 121. Pages 5, 10, 11, 17 were especially informative.

The preface explicitly frames the Fuxi Eight Trigrams and 64 hexagrams as mathematical documents / symbol-number tables rather than merely divinatory marks.

Core claims extracted from page 5 and pages 10-17:

```text
八卦 = eight symbol digits.
六十四卦 = sixty-four symbol digits/states.
Broken line = 0.
Solid line = 1.
Two symbols combine into three-position arrays.
Three-position arrays produce eight symbols/digits.
Eight trigrams serve as binary 3-place symbols and octal 1-place symbols.
Sixty-four hexagrams serve as binary 6-place symbols and octal 2-place symbols.
Counting table is finite, deductive/inference space is unbounded.
The tables contain counting method, arithmetic operation method, and extraction/root method.
```

Representative OCR fragments:

```text
"八卦，既是二进制三位数符号数字计数表，又是八进制一位数符号数字计数表"

"六十四卦，既是二进制六位数符号数字计数表，又是八进制两位数符号数字计数表"

"计数表有限，演绎空间无穷"
```

### Normalized mechanism

```text
retained carrier:

  same retained carrier supports binary, octal, decimal, base-64, and computer-counting comparisons.
```


That maps directly onto the project question:

The answer for this source is: **strong candidate, not closed**.
