  1. Phase Calculus: the name of the whole system.

operator core.

4.2      Methodological Clarification: Compute in the Lift, Project at
         the Boundary
The central methodological lesson of the unified work is that Phase Calculus is not a way of
performing a scalar search inside an already projected mathematical space. It is a retained-state
transport discipline. The visible witness is not state-complete. Exact return cannot be decided at
the visible layer, and an exact solver cannot be built from visible witness alone. The lifted state is
therefore not auxiliary bookkeeping wrapped around a calculation. It is the native computational
arena.
   In practical terms, the computation must remain in the fully lifted state throughout the entire
hierarchical descent. One does not alternate between lift and projection at intermediate stages.
One initializes a lifted state, evolves it only by the admissible lifted operators, preserves every
carried coordinate that distinguishes recurrence from identity and transport from coincidence, and
postpones projection until the retained burden has been transported and tightened to the required
precision. For the primitive lifted object this means carrying

                                         Ξ̂ = (A, q, θ, κ, c).

For branch-complete problems such as the generic quintic this means carrying

                                      Ξ̂P = (A, q, θ, κ, c, s, h),

so that sheet identity and branch history remain internal to the computation rather than being
discarded and later re-inferred.

CHAPTER 4. EXACT LIFTED OBJECT                                                          Phase Calculus


   This also clarifies the role of hierarchical descent. The hierarchy is not merely a proof presentation
or a post hoc organization of approximants. It is the control law of the computation itself. On the
executable branch the exact step always advances the lifted phase and then, still inside the retained
state, either refines, continues, or host-lifts. The selector-closed macro-calculus makes precise that
there is one engine and no external scheduler. Hierarchical descent is therefore the mechanism by
which retained structure is preserved while resolution increases.
   Once this is seen, the same architecture explains both π and the quintic. The Fibonacci corridor
does not carry a special instruction for “compute π” or “compute a quintic root.” It walks the lifted
state. The target appears only when a projector is finally applied. In the quintic case, the root
projector is a fifth filter satisfying the same commutation law as the other Phase Calculus filters:

                                           Π ◦ E = G ◦ Π.

The retained transport acts first and the root readout acts second. The resulting certificate is
therefore not a log of scalar approximations drifting toward a target. It is the projected descendant
of a retained transport that has already located the root before scalar collapse.
   This is why Abel–Ruffini does not obstruct the method. The obstruction belongs to the scalar
projection layer, where branch memory, completed-turn memory, germ location, and sheet history
have already been discarded. Phase Calculus does not ask that impoverished layer to reconstruct
what it no longer carries. It keeps the full burden in the lift until the end, and only then reads the
scalar descendant.
   The same point resolves the discrete/continuous tension. Phase Calculus operates by exact
discrete means in lifted state; continuity is recovered as a shadow readout of that exact transport
rather than presupposed as the native workspace. The continuous plane, the certified root, the
streamed digit block, or any later domain object is not where the computation fundamentally
lives. It is what becomes visible when a completed lifted evolution is read through the appropriate
projector.
   Accordingly, the method can be stated in one sentence: remain in the fully lifted state for the
entire computation, perform the full hierarchical descent there, and project only at the boundary
where the carried burden has already been resolved.
   This retained-first discipline is illustrated downstream by branch-specific worked examples,
including quintic root certification by lifted transport and terminal projection.
