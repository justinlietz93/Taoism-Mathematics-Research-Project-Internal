## Paper thesis

James A. Ryan's paper argues that the Leibniz/Shao Yong/Yijing binary episode should not be reduced to either:

```text
1. proof of forgotten ancient Chinese binary mathematics, or
2. mere coincidence with no deeper structural significance.
```

The sharper reading is:

```text
Leibniz identified the Fu Xi/Shao Yong hexagram diagram as binary because, under a particular readout convention, it displays the same double geometric progression as his binary number system.

Shao Yong did not use it as a modern number system.

But Shao did use the diagram in a proto-scientific, mechanistic, correlative, finite-position way: the binary ordering was treated as a cosmological structure that organizes natural kinds, cyclic change, and waxing/waning.
```
